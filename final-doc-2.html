Here's a new conclusion paragraph:

"I've found the best way to write is to simply take things one step at a time. Don't start by writing a final draft, as it'll kill my motivation (and I bet yours too) when I inevitably have a hard time doing so. It's similar to formality and worrying about technical format. That's not really a part of the writing process and only matters after the fact. Don't let it get in the way of effective communication. The most important part of writing is conveying your message. To convey that message, you must be able to draw from various perspectives and be able to show that you have an understanding deeper than surface level. To make that not the biggest pain in the butt in the world, I think you should refuse to be a perfectionist; let Lamott's idea of the inner writing child guide you through the process instead. And hey! Maybe it isn't such a bad idea to take a couple breaks."

Not only is this a smoother way of wrapping up the essay, it also expands upon the thesis a bit, that it always kills me when I insist upon being a perfectionist and that no one can be able to see me do something incorrect.
